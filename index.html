Mission

The Stillpoint Foundation restores equilibrium where the world has lost its rhythm—through rapid humanitarian relief, ethical education, and long‑arc reconstruction. Our endowment is held in STLPNT, a sovereign digital reserve designed for measurable human good. We do not solicit public donations; instead, we convert a fixed share of our endowment each year into direct, auditable outcomes delivered with trusted local partners.

Restoration Grants

We fund response and recovery that is fast, local, and lasting. Grants target the period between acute crisis and long‑term development—when communities are most vulnerable to secondary harm.

  • Rapid Window: Disburse within 72 hours for logistics, medical supplies, clean water, and emergency shelter.
  • Local First: Purchase from regional suppliers to accelerate delivery and stimulate the recovery economy.
  • Build Back Better: Reconstruction uses climate‑resilient designs and community stewardship models.
  • Transparent by Default: All tranches and milestones mirrored to a public, read‑only ledger.

Continuum Scholarships

Education is how recovery continues after the news cycle ends. We invest in people who will hold communities together long after the grant closes.

  • Fellowships: Graduate and professional funding for ethics of technology, public health, climate adaptation, and cultural memory.
  • Chairs & Residencies: Co‑funded posts at universities and teaching hospitals to anchor expertise locally.
  • Open Learning: Multilingual curricula and toolkits released under open licences for schools and NGOs.

Equilibrium Health

Health systems are the spine of recovery. We support mobile and fixed care that is culturally safe and designed to be owned locally.

  • Mobile Clinics: Primary care, maternal health, and trauma‑informed mental‑health services.
  • Cold‑Chain & Diagnostics: Solar refrigeration, point‑of‑care testing, and telemedicine hubs.
  • Capacity: Training pathways for community health workers, with certification and supervision.

What We Fund

We fund programmes that reduce avoidable suffering and increase community agency. Every grant links to a concrete outcome that can be photographed, audited, or independently verified.

  • Emergency Relief: Food rations, cash‑for‑work, water purification, WASH kits, emergency shelter materials, and protection services.
  • Reconstruction: Climate‑resilient housing, flood‑safe water systems, off‑grid energy, and repair of public assets such as classrooms and clinics.
  • Health Systems: Mobile units, essential medicines lists, diagnostics, referral networks, and mental‑health programmes for survivors and staff.
  • Education: Teacher training, digital and print curricula, scholarships for educators and health workers, and school‑in‑a‑box deployments.
  • Cultural & Environmental Preservation: Digitisation of archives, community museums, mangrove and watershed restoration, and local ranger training.

Funding Model

Our endowment is denominated in STLPNT. Each year, a fixed share is converted to operational currency. This maintains inter‑generational capacity while ensuring decisive action now.

  • Endowment: 27,984,549.08348 STLPNT with transparent supply and custody.
  • Annual Release: 0.5% of endowment disbursed into programmes, balanced across regions and sectors.
  • Overheads: ≤ 1% of annual release, emphasising automation, open tooling, and partner‑led delivery.
  • Re‑absorption: Unspent allocations return automatically to the Stillness Vault at the solstice.
  • Price Reference: Grants contracted in local currency; public dashboard shows STLPNT valuation translated via live FX.

Governance & Accountability

Trust in humanitarian work is earned through clarity. We publish how decisions are made, who benefits, and what changes because of the work—not just what we spent.

  • Board of Stewards: Seven trustees with expertise across humanitarian operations, public health, finance, ethics, and law. Conflicts of interest are disclosed and managed publicly.
  • Advisory Council: A rotating panel of field practitioners, cryptographers, clinicians, educators, and community leaders who review pipeline proposals.
  • On‑Chain Transparency: Every grant milestone appears on a public, read‑only ledger with narrative context and a unique verification hash.
  • Independent Review: An annual Stillpoint Report summarises impact metrics, lessons learned, and course corrections—archived in perpetuity.
  • Whistleblowing & Safeguarding: Secure channels for concerns, survivor‑centred responses, and legal follow‑through where required.

Grantmaking

We design with communities, not for them. Our process ensures speed when it matters and patience when the right answer takes time.

  1. Partner Qualification: Due diligence on governance, financial controls, safeguarding, and local legitimacy.
  2. Needs Assessment: Community consultation that centres women, youth, and people with disabilities.
  3. Design & Budget: Milestone‑based plans with measurable outcomes and exit strategies.
  4. On‑Chain Disbursement: Tranches released against verified milestones; variance management where conditions change.
  5. Monitoring & Learning: Mixed‑methods evaluation, independent verification, and publication of findings.

Initial Regions

Our first portfolio balances disaster‑prone geographies with opportunities for durable impact, in partnership with organisations already trusted on the ground.

  • Aegean Coast: Climate refugee resettlement with local municipalities—secure shelter, language access, and job‑matching services.
  • Sudan–Chad: Mobile health corridors bridging camps and host communities; training community health workers and establishing referral networks.
  • Indonesia: Earthquake‑resilient reconstruction using local materials and artisans; community‑owned micro‑insurance.
  • Northern Pakistan: Scholarships for women in STEM and teacher‑training in remote valleys; safe transport and housing.
  • Vanuatu & Kiribati: Renewable microgrids, freshwater security, and cultural digitisation before climate loss accelerates.

Ethics & Safeguards

Humanitarian action must never become a source of harm. Our safeguards are practical, enforceable, and transparent.

  • Do‑No‑Harm Design: Context analysis and risk registers accompany every grant.
  • Safeguarding: Survivor‑centred policies; mandatory reporting; background checks for personnel.
  • Environment: Minimum standards on emissions, materials, and biodiversity impacts.
  • Data Responsibility: Privacy‑by‑design; no surveillance; encryption for sensitive information.
  • Local Ownership: Handover plans and community governance baked into project charters.

Eligibility & How to Apply

We work through trusted organisations with the legitimacy to lead. If that describes you, we’re ready to listen.

  • Registered NGOs, public institutions, or community trusts with clean audits or verifiable financials.
  • Two‑page concept note; if shortlisted, an invited full proposal with budget and risk plan.
  • Proof of community leadership and a credible exit to local stewardship.
  • Clear, measurable outcomes and a commitment to public reporting.
  • Send proposals to grants@stillpoint.foundation

FAQ

How is STLPNT valued? The Foundation maintains a canonical NZD valuation per token. This page converts that figure to USD using public NZD→USD foreign‑exchange rates in real time, so partners can plan in their operating currency.

Do you accept donations? No. The endowment is sufficient for our mandate. We welcome knowledge, local leadership, and courage more than cash.

How do I track a grant? Each grant has a public reference on our read‑only ledger mirror with milestones, outputs, and verification notes.

What is your overhead? Administrative overhead is capped at ≤1% of annual programme release and disclosed in the Stillpoint Report.
